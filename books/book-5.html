The Economic Way of Thinking

Economics. Statistics. Applications.

Overview

This textbook introduces the fundamental principles of microeconomics by focusing on the logical framework of economic reasoning rather than heavy mathematical modeling. It equips readers with the "economic way of thinking," a critical tool for analyzing individual choices, social institutions, and market dynamics. Through real-world examples, the book illustrates how incentives and opportunity costs shape human behavior in everyday life. It is designed to provide a solid conceptual foundation for understanding how free markets and government policies interact.
